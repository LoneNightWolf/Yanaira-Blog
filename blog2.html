Computational Artifact

2a:

Netflix is a media company that allows users to access their content online and stream it to various devices.[1] The goal of the stream service was for the content to always be available and affordable.[2] The first four sections of the artifact show how Netflix gains its content and distributes it to various devices all over the world by utilizing AWS servers and freely providing Netflix Open Connection to Internet Service Providers. The last section shows how the Netflix App utilizes Open Connection to provide the user with the best quality video in a short amount of time.

2c:

The benefits and harmful effects that Netflix has created are seen in the Netflix binge culture and its impact on rental services. Unlike regular television, Netflix releases full seasons of a show at once.[3] This has led to the creation of the Netflix binge culture because members are able to instantly watch all the episodes of a series. Netflix is also commercial free and you are able to download content and watch it offline.[4] The benefit is that it allows people to have quick and easy access to their favorite tv shows and movies, as they do not have to wait for the following episode, for the commercials to end, or to get home. However, the Netflix binge culture is harmful as it has decreased the amount of patience and has led to the feelings of entitlement in the new generation. Netflix’s streaming service allowed people to watch tv shows, movies, and Netflix originals wherever they go and at any time that they desired.[5] The benefit was that people no longer needed to make the effort to rent a movie or a series that might not be available at their rental places. The rise of Netflix led to Blockbuster going bankrupt and Redbox losing customers.[6] The harmful effect was that it caused Blockbuster to lose customers to the point it went bankrupt and it took customers away from Redbox, as people no longer needed to go to a rental service to find movies and shows.

2d:

Netflix has been able to become successful due to its recommendation system that was created by an algorithm that gathers the customers data. Netflix’s algorithm gathers ratings, searches, date the movie or show was watched, the device used, nature of the shows or movies watched on the different devices, times the program is paused, parts that get re-watched, and if the credits are skipped.[7] This has allowed Netflix to discover and produce movies and shows that appeal to their customers. It has also changed the way users interact with Netflix on their devices. The algorithm has led to the profile of every user being unique as it organizes the content available to appeal to what the users like to watch. Netflix makes personal recommendation based on the user’s data, genres shown differ from user to user, contains a section on what is trending, and has a section that allows the user to continue watching shows or movies that they did not finish.[7] Netflix’s big data on their members activities has led to the personalization of each members’ profile. One concern that comes from Netflix is not how people’s information would get stolen, but how Netflix can utilize the data they gather through their algorithm. Netflix does not have the issue of member’s personal information being stolen, as they do not sell their member’s data to advertisers.[8] The problem isn’t data security, it’s data privacy. Netflix recommendation system allows them to shift and control the way people think.
